About Al-Zahraa Centre

Learn more about our mission, values, and the people who serve Sussex’s Shia Muslim community.

Our Mission & Vision

Al-Zahraa Centre exists to provide a welcoming home for Shia Muslims and friends of Ahlul Bayt (AS) across Brighton, Hove, and Sussex. We aim to nurture spiritual growth, moral development, and a strong sense of community rooted in the teachings of the Holy Prophet (PBUH) and his pure Household (AS).

As an English-speaking centre, we strive to bridge generations and cultures, helping those who grew up in the UK, new Muslims, and seekers connect deeply with their faith in a language they understand.

Our Strategic Approach

  • English-first programmes to ensure accessibility for all backgrounds.
  • Inclusive community where ethnicity and language never limit belonging.
  • Sussex-wide reach for anyone seeking spiritual connection and community support.
  • Cultural bridge that honours heritage while engaging with wider society.

Our Core Values

  • Love and loyalty to Ahlul Bayt (AS)
  • Accessible Islamic education in English
  • Spiritual growth and moral refinement
  • Service, compassion, and community support
  • Interfaith understanding and positive engagement
  • Youth empowerment and responsible leadership

Our Story

Al-Zahraa Centre was established in response to a growing need for a dedicated Shia community space in Brighton & Hove. Many local families, students, and professionals were travelling long distances to attend programmes or felt there was no space that truly reflected their needs.

Named after Sayyida Fatimah al-Zahraa (AS), the beloved daughter of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH), our centre aspires to embody her qualities of faith, knowledge, modesty, and service. From our earliest gatherings, the vision was clear: a non-ethnocentric, English-speaking centre that welcomes people from all walks of life.

Today, Al-Zahraa Centre hosts religious observances, community gatherings, and developing educational programmes, while continuing to grow through the support of local families, students, and volunteers.

Our Facilities

We are continually improving our space so that it remains welcoming, dignified and fit-for-purpose for worship and community life.

Prayer Spaces

Separate prayer areas for men and women, providing a peaceful environment for daily worship and religious programmes.

Community Hall

A flexible hall used for religious observances, community meals, lectures, and social gatherings.

Event Space

Space that can be adapted for lectures, workshops, youth sessions, and private community functions.
